automotive diesel fuel

Automotive diesel fuel is a refined petroleum distillate, primarily hydrocarbons, used in compression-ignition engines for vehicles like cars, trucks, trains, and buses, known for its energy efficiency and high torque, with modern formulations featuring low sulfur content (like 50ppm or 10ppm) and additives for lubrication, detergents, and cold-weather performance, adhering to national standards like SANS 342 in South Africa for quality.

Aviation Fuel(A1Jet)

Aviation Fuel (Jet A-1) is a globally used, kerosene-based fuel for turbine (jet and turboprop) aircraft, characterized by a low freezing point (max -47°C) for cold high-altitude flight and a minimum flash point (38°C) for safety, containing additives for static dissipation, detergency, and preventing microbial growth, making it the international standard for most commercial aviation outside the US.

Illuminating Paraffin

Illuminating paraffin (IP), also known as kerosene, is a refined fuel oil used primarily for lighting, heating, and cooking, especially in areas with limited electricity, burning with a bright, clean flame in lamps and stoves. It's a vital energy source in many parts of Africa, offering efficient combustion and low sulphur content, but it's also flammable and toxic, requiring careful handling, with safety concerns around its use in poorly maintained appliances.
